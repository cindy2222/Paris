Paris Ossuaries and Museums

Le Louvre or Musee du Louvre, is the world's largest museum and a historic monument in Paris, France. A central landmark of the city, it is located on the Right Bank of the Seine in the city's 1st arrondissement (district or ward). Approximately 38,000 objects from prehistory to the 21st century are exhibited. The museum is housed in the Louvre Palace, originally built as a fortress in the late 12th century under Philip II. The museum opened on 10 August 1793 with an exhibition of 537 paintings, the majority of the works being royal and confiscated church property. Pictured is the Louvre Pyramid outside of the museum. Other musuems of Paris, France include: Musee d'Orsay, Grand Palais (de Champs Elysees), Les Invalides, Musee Rodin, Musee de cluny-Musee National du Moyen Age, Musee de l'Orangerie, Musee du quai Branly-Jacques Chirac, Musee Picasso, Petit Palais, Musee Jaquemart-Andre. Paris' Ossuaries are the Catacombs of Paris and Pere Lachaise cemetery.

Paris Gardens and Parks

Disneyland Paris and Walt Disney Studios Park, was originally called Euro Disney Resort. It is the most visited theme park in all of Europe. It is and is the only resort outside of the United States to be owned and operated by The Walt Disney Company (Through its wholly owned subsidiary Euro Disney S.C.A). It encompasses two theme parks (Disneyland Park and Walt Disney Studios Park), a golf course, many resort hotels, a shopping/dining/entertainment complex, and several recreation/entertainment venues. The resort is the second Disney park to open outside of the United States (second to Tokyo Disney Resort in 1983). Other Paris theme parks are: Auquaboulevard de Paris, Yoo Moov Stations, Jardin d'Acclimatation, Aerophile, France Miniature, Poussin Vert, Lock Busters, Carousel of the Eiffel Tower, Asterix Park, Escape Game Paris Prisoners, HintHunt Live Escape Game, and Playmobile Fun Park. Paris park gardens include: Tuileries Garden, Jardin du Luxembourg, Champs de Mars, Parc de Buttes Chaumont, Parc de la Villette, and Parc Monceau.

Paris Islands, Canals, Bridges, and Boardwalks

The Pont des Arts or Passerelle des Arts is a pedestrian bridge in Paris which crosses the River Seine. It links the Institut de France and the central square (cour carree) of the Palais du Louvre, (which had been termed the "Palais des Arts" under the First French Empire). In Paris, there are 37 bridges over the Seine River, 148 bridges over the boulevard peripherique (sometimes called Periph', is a controlled-access dual-carriageway ring road in Paris), 49 pedestrian bridges or "passerelles pietonnieres", which are footbridges, and several bridges which do not cross a body of water. Paris canals include: Canal Saint Martin and Canal de l'Ourcq. Paris islands are: Ile de la cite and Ile Saint-Louis. Paris boardwalks include: Champs-Elysees, Place de la Concorde, Latin Quarter, Paris, Place de Vosges, Place de la Bastille, Saint German des Pres, and Rue de Rivoli.

Paris Monuments

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower. Constructed from 1887-89 as the centrepiece for the proposed 1889 Exposition Universelle, a world's fair to celebrate the centennial of the French Revolution World's Fair. Eiffel openly acknowledged that inspiration for a tower came from the Latting Observatory built in New York City in 1853. The Eiffel Tower is the most-visited paid monument in the world. The tower is about the same height as an 81-storey building, and the tallest structure in Paris. Other Paris monuments include:Arc de Triomphe, Flamme de la Liberte, Colonne de Juillet, Luxor Obelisk, Monument aux Meres Frances Garden, Porte Saint-Martin, and Monuments de droite de la Homme.

Paris Churches and Palaces

Sacre-Coeur (Basilica of the Sacred Heart of Paris, commonly known as Sacre-Coeur Basilica) is a Roman Catholic church and minor basilica, dedicated to the Sacred Heart of Jesus, in Paris, France. A popular landmark, the basilica is located at the summit of the butte Montmartre, the highest point in the city. Sacre-Coeur is a double monument, political and cultural, both a national penance for the defeat of France in the 1870 Franco-Prussian War and the socialist Paris Commune of 1871, publicly dedicated to the Sacred Heart of Jesus. It was consecrated after the end of World War I in 1919. Sacre-Coeur is asserted to be dedicated in honor of the 58,000 who lost their lives during the Franco Prussian war, which lasted from 1870 to 1871. Other Paris churches, chapels and cathedrals include: Notre-Dame de Paris, and Sainte-Chapelle. Palaces in Paris include: Grand Palais, Palais Royal, Petit Palais and Louvre Palace (at the Louvre).
